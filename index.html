Edward Hopper

1882 - 1967

This tribute page is dedicated to the American painter Edward Hopper

Learn More
Nighthawks, 1942, Art Institute of Chicago

Timeline

1882

  • Birth

  • Edward Hopper was born on July 22 1882 in Nymack, New York from middle class parents.
  • He signed his first oil painting at the age of 13.
1900

  • School & Travel

  • From 1900 to 1906, Hopper studies at the New York School of Arts also learning illustration.
  • Between 1906 and 1910, he travels to Europe but mainly to Paris. The Impressionists will have a great influence in his work.
1913

  • Struggle

  • Hopper struggled to gain recognition before selling his first painting Sailing (1911) at the Armory Show in New York in 1913.
  • He will work as an illustrator before making his breakthrough.
1923

  • Breakthrough

  • Since 1920, Edward Hopper is able to showcase his Art through exhibits.
  • In 1923, his medium shifts to watercolour for which he receives the Logen Prize from the Chicago Society of Etchers. That same year, he marries Josephine Nivision a fellow student from the NY School of Arts.
  • House by the Railroad (1925) will be his first painting acquired by The Museum of Modern Art, New York.
1930 - 1940

  • Rural America

  • Edward Hopper and his wife will frequently travel around America particularly in Massachussetts. Hopper depicts the tension between interior and exterior environments as well as light.
  • Loneliness becomes a central theme of his work. His paintings are recognised as contemporary and praises his style.
1942

  • Urban Life

  • Nighthawks (1942) illustrates the observation Hopper has made of the solitude found in urban life.
  • Women working will be also be a source of inspiration for Hopper's artwork.
  • Hopper finds more commercial success with his paintings.
1950s

  • Major Works

  • Although Abstract Expressionists will cast shadow on the style of Hopper, it is during this era that he will create his major works such as Morning Sun (1952) or Sunlight on Brownstones (1956).
  • In 1953, he will become Doctor of Fine Arts at the Institute of Chicago and receive numerous and prestigeous prizes.
1967

  • Death

  • His last painting Two Comedians (1965) illustrates him and his wife.
  • Edward Hopper dies on May 15 1967.
  • His wife Josephine donated the majority of work to the Whitney Museum at her death, less than a year later.
